No man is an
island.  entire of itself;

every man is a
piece of the continent, a part of the main;

if a clod be
washed away by the sea,

Europe is the
less,

as well as if a
promontory were,

as well as if a
manor of thy friend’s

or of thine own
were;

any man’s death
diminishes me,

because I am
involved in mankind,

and therefore
never send to know for whom the bell tolls;

it tolls for
thee.

 

                     
–By John
Donne
